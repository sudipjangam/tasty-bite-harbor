# 🍔 Food Truck Management System - User Manual

Welcome to your complete platform guide! This manual is designed to help you—whether you're a food truck owner, a manager, or a new staff member—learn everything you need to know to run your food truck smoothly using our software. 

By the end of this guide, you will be able to confidently navigate the system, take orders, manage your menu, track your inventory, and generate sales reports.

---

## 1. Introduction
**What is the Food Truck System?**  
The Food Truck System is an all-in-one digital platform built to handle the daily operations of your food truck. It replaces manual paper tickets and basic cash registers with a smart system that tracks everything in real-time.

**Who is this for?**
* **Owners/Managers:** To track overall performance, update the menu, and generate financial reports.
* **Cashiers/Front Staff:** To punch in orders, manage customers, and process payments.
* **Kitchen Staff:** To view incoming orders and mark them as prepared.

## 5. Orders Management (Point of Sale)
This is where your staff will spend most of their time. The Orders Management screen allows you to capture customer orders quickly.

**What does it do?**  
It lets you select items, apply discounts, calculate totals, and process payments.

### Step-by-Step: Taking a New Order
1. Click on **Orders** from the Navigation Menu.
2. You will see a grid of your menu items. Tap on the item(s) the customer wants to order.
3. *Optional:* If a customer wants customizations (e.g., "No Onions", "Extra Cheese"), click the item in the cart to add a note or modifier.
4. Review the **Cart Summary** on the right side of the screen to ensure the order is correct.
5. Click the big **Checkout / Pay** button.
6. Select the payment method (**Cash**, **Card**, or **Digital Wallet**).
7. Click **Complete Order**. The order will automatically be sent to the Kitchen Display!

> 🖼️ **[Insert Screenshot Here: The POS Order screen showing menu item buttons and the right-hand cart summary]**

### Step-by-Step: Managing Kitchen Orders (KDS)
1. Go to the **Active Orders** or **Kitchen Display** tab.
2. New orders will appear as cards with the requested items.
3. Once the food is ready, click **Mark as Done** so the front staff knows to hand it to the customer.

## 6. Quick Serve POS
Sometimes you are dealing with a huge rush and need a simplified interface to pump out fast transactions, especially for drinks or pre-made grabs.

**What does it do?**  
The Quick Serve POS is a specialized, streamlined version of the standard order screen. It is designed purely for speed and high-volume transactions, skipping complex menus and customization steps.

### Step-by-Step: Processing a Quick Serve Order
1. Click on **Quick Serve** from the Navigation Menu (or switch to Quick Serve mode from the main POS).
2. The interface will display a simplified grid of large, easy-to-tap buttons for your most popular or ready-to-hand-out items.
3. Tap the items to instantly add them to the running checkout total.
4. Click **Pay / Checkout**.
5. Select the payment method and complete the transaction. (Since it's built for speed, this flow minimizes the number of clicks required!)

## 7. Menu / Product Management
Need to add a new seasonal taco or change the price of your burgers? You will do it here.

**What does it do?**  
Allows managers to add, remove, and edit the items that appear on the ordering screen.

### Step-by-Step: Adding a New Menu Item
1. Click on **Menu** from the Navigation Menu.
2. Click the **+ Add New Item** button (usually at the top right).
3. Fill out the details:
   * **Item Name:** (e.g., "Spicy BBQ Burger")
   * **Category:** (e.g., "Main Course", "Drinks")
   * **Price:** Enter the selling price.
   * **Image:** Upload an appetizing photo.
4. Click **Save Changes**. The item will instantly appear on the POS for your cashiers.

## 8. Inventory Management
Never run out of crucial ingredients during a busy lunch rush again!

**What does it do?**  
Tracks your raw ingredients and supplies. The system warns you when you are running low on specific items.

### Step-by-Step: Updating Stock Levels
1. Click on **Inventory** from the Navigation Menu.
2. You will see a list of your tracked ingredients (e.g., "Burger Buns", "Soda Cans").
3. Find the item you just restocked and click **Edit** or **Update Stock**.
4. Enter the new quantity you have on hand.
5. Watch out for items highlighted in **Red**—this means they have fallen below the "Low Stock Warning" limit and need to be ordered from your supplier!

## 9. Customer Management
Building a loyal customer base is vital for a food truck. 

**What does it do?**  
Allows you to save phone numbers, names, and email addresses to track frequent visitors and send them receipts or loyalty discounts.

### Step-by-Step: Adding a Customer During an Order
1. On the Orders (POS) screen, before clicking Checkout, click on **Add Customer**.
2. Ask the customer for their phone number. If they are in the system, their name will pop up.
3. If they are new, quickly type in their **Name** and **Phone Number** and click **Save**.
4. Now, the order will be linked to their profile, and you can easily send them an SMS receipt!

## 10. Sales Tracking
At the end of a shift, you need to know exactly how much cash should be in the drawer.

**What does it do?**  
Tracks daily registers, cash flow, and payment types across specific shifts.

### Step-by-Step: Closing out the Register / Shift
1. Click on **Sales** from the Navigation Menu.
2. View the summary of your current shift. It will show expected Cash vs. Card totals.
3. Count the physical cash in your drawer.
4. Click **Close Shift**.
5. Enter the exact amount of cash you counted. If there is a difference between the system and the drawer, it will log a discrepancy note for the manager.

## 11. Reports & Analytics
This section is for managers and owners to make smart business decisions. 

**What does it do?**  
Generates downloadable, detailed reports (PDF or Excel worksheet) over any time period (today, this week, last month).

### Step-by-Step: Generating a Weekly Sales Report
1. Click on **Reports** from the Navigation Menu.
2. Select the type of report you want (e.g., **"Sales Overview"** or **"Item Performance"**).
3. Use the **Date Filter** at the top to select "Last 7 Days".
4. The screen will display beautiful charts of your peak sales hours and top-selling items.
5. To save this for your records, click **Export** at the top right and choose **PDF** or **Excel**.

## 12. Settings & Configuration
Tailor the system to perfectly fit your specific food truck's needs.

**What does it do?**  
Controls background details like tax rates, receipts, user permissions, and hardware (like receipt printers).

### Step-by-Step: Changing the Tax Rate
1. Click on **Settings** from the Navigation Menu.
2. Click on the **Billing / Taxes** tab.
3. Locate the Tax Rate field and update the percentage (e.g., from `5%` to `8%`).
4. Click **Save Configuration**. The new tax rate will immediately apply to all new orders.

## 13. Troubleshooting / FAQs
Having a minor issue? Check here before calling support.

**Q: My POS screen is frozen during an order, what do I do?**
> A: Try hard-refreshing your browser (Ctrl + R or swiping down on a tablet). If that doesn't work, close the app and reopen it. Unsaved items in the cart might clear, but your completed orders are perfectly safe.

**Q: Why isn't the receipt printer working?**
> A: Check the following: 
> 1. Is the printer turned on and plugged into power? 
> 2. Does it have paper? 
> 3. Go to **Settings > Hardware** and ensure the printer status says "Connected".

**Q: An item says "Out of Stock" but we just bought more!**
> A: Head over to the **Inventory** tab, find the item, and update the quantity to reflect your recent purchase. Return to the POS, and it will be available to sell again.

**Q: How do I refund an order a customer canceled?**
> A: Go to the **Sales** tab, locate the specific order from the list, click on it to open the details, and select the red **Refund / Cancel Order** button at the bottom.
